What is an Input-Output Table?

An input-output (I-O) table is a matrix that records how much each industry in an economy buys from every other industry — and from final demand (households, government, exports). Developed by Nobel laureate Wassily Leontief in the 1940s, I-O analysis lets economists answer questions like: if auto sales rise by $1 billion, how much does steel output need to grow?

The U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) publishes annual I-O accounts at the summary (71-sector) level. This site packages every table from 1997 to 2024 into machine-readable formats and derived matrices — all freely downloadable and reproducible.
